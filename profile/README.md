Theoretica is a young collective of physicists, mathematicians, and programmers who are passionate about pushing the boundaries of open research and development in science through scientific computing. We want to make knowledge accessible to everyone, our goal is to create open‑source tools and resources that facilitate it.

## Projects
- [**Theoretica Math**](https://github.com/chaotic-society/theoretica)
  > A modular and header-only library in C++ for scientific computing, with numerical methods of wide use.

- [**Chebyshev Test**](http://github.com/chaotic-society/chebyshev)
  > A unit testing and numerical validation framework for scientific software.

### Exploratory Projects
- [**Theoretica Lab**](http://github.com/chaotic-society/theoretica-lab)
  > A place to explore new ideas and theories together.

- [**Metropolis**](http://github.com/chaotic-society/metropolis)
  > The Metropolis sampling algorithm and its variants.

## Our Team
We are composed of students and researchers who are eager to create innovative, open solutions for scientific computing. From building tools for numerical simulations to implementing complex algorithms, our work is about making cutting-edge scientific research more accessible. We support the idea that research results and knowledge should be available to everyone.

## Our Principles
By offering our software under open-source licenses, we invite everyone to contribute, learn, and build upon the projects we create. We envision a future where science is **open**, **accessible**, and fueled by the **collective intelligence** of its practitioners. Together, we try to shape a community that thrives on collaboration and shared discovery.
